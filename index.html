FBDialogue A

Once upon a time I had a real conversation on Facebook and it felt good.

FBDialogue is a browser extension aiming to simulate a real life conversation. 
Any interaction happening on Facebook is today shaped by the standard, black-or-white options the social network is giving to you: would our Facebook conversations change, if we had to express ourselves just with words -no emoji, no GIFs allowed?
Would they change, also, if we really had to listen to other people - with a slow pace, one at a time? And what if we had no chance to delete or rethink what we just wrote - just as we can’t go back in time and change what we just said out loud?

FBDialogue makes everything on the social network that is not based on conceiving, reading, and commenting contents almost useless and unreachable.
It does not involve any pre-conceived expression (there’s no “I like it”, no “grr” reactions).
It makes listening and thinking quite necessary.
It makes endless scrolling quite useless and impossible.

At the place of contents, your feed consists of blank spaces, each one marking a post. Every post gets visible when you go over it, and disappears when you move to the following one. This means you can only read one content at a time: just as in real life you can’t listen to everything simultaneously. Comments follow the same logic: to listen to somebody, you’ll have to stop and pause every other noise.  If you wish to join the conversation, you’ll have to think of what you want to say first: in fact, you won’t be able to see what you are writing while you’re typing, but only once it’s published.
This way, a thoughtless comment is disencouraged, and so is a useless fight: if reading and writing is taking a bit of effort, you don’t want to waste your time posting and reading bullshit, do you?
